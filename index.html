About

Computer Society of India, MJCET and E-CELL, MJCET have always tried to incite the innovative and competitive spirit of students since 2014 through 'Hackathon', a marathon where you spend a period of time building, creating and delivering a product to the best of your team's creative and technical ability. The idea is to collaboratively code in an extreme manner, to start from scratch and end with a working prototype which can confirm with the problem statement given and the domain selected. Problem statements will be regarding the issues and scenarios faced in real time by the general public in various sectors like Medicine, Transportation, Crime , Safety, Commerce, etc.

Previous Hackathons

Click / Touch on the image to expand.

  • MJ-HACK 2017

    On 8th October 2017, at Ghulam Ahmed Hall, MJCET we have organised "MJ-Hack", a 12 hour hackathon from 8:30 am to 8:30 pm and received enormous response from 150 participants from different branches of various engineering colleges across the state to showcase their talent in various trending domains of IT sector like E-Commerce, Chatbots, Application development, Internet Of Things, Website Cloning, Home Automation and Cyber Security and have even grabbed lucrative rewards on winning and have exhibited their prowess before judges from various start-ups like Bot-hook , Iftar move and also Mozilla Firefox which was the technical sponsor for this event.

  • XtremeHack 2016

    The 12 hour long extravaganza organized by Computer Society of Inida (CSI)-MJCET partnering up with XtreamIT saw a quality turnout from various colleges from across the map of Hyderabad! App and web development were the main theme for this Hackathon.

  • Mozilla Hackathon 2015

    The department of Computer Science, Muffakham Jah College of Engineering and Technology and CSI-MJCET in collaboration with Mozilla Firefox succesfully organized a 12hr programming event, “Mozilla Hackathon” on 25th January 2015. The theme for the hackathon was “Creating Jobs for Millions”. The event aimed to bring together technology and the spirit of entrepreneurship, by organizing a Web and App development competition for the Firefox OS framework.

  • Google Hackathon 2014

    Google Student Club VCE collaborated with Computer Society Of India (CSI) -MJCET to conduct "India's 1st Digital Footprint" in Hyderabad, a Hackathon for the student web designers to develop a website for real time business within 12 hrs on 23rd February 2014 at MJCET, Hyderabad. Their websites were reviewed and mentored by developers from the Google Developer Group (GDG) - Hyderabad and also the clients of the websites.
